Designs and Groups: constructions and classifications

Designs and Groups is an online database of nontrivial \(2\)-designs admitting highly symmetric automorphism groups. It brings together constructions, classification results, structural information, and data readable by GAP (Groups, Algorithms, and Programming) in a common framework.

The database is organised around two complementary collections: flag-transitive designs and block-transitive designs. Within each collection, entries are arranged by group family and linked to computational data files, allowing users to move directly from group-level classification summaries to the underlying GAP data.

Block-transitive designs

Designs with an automorphism group transitive on the set of blocks.

Browse the catalogue
The block designs recorded here are simple (no repeated blocks), binary (no repeated points within a block), and pairwise balanced: every pair of distinct points lies in exactly \(\lambda\) blocks.

Scholarly attribution

If you use Designs and Groups in the preparation of a paper, preprint, thesis, presentation, software package, or related scholarly work, please cite the database. For work based on particular constructions or classifications, please also cite the relevant original sources in the Bibliography.

The references provided are not intended to be exhaustive, and the inclusion of a reference does not necessarily indicate the first publication in which the corresponding design was constructed.
