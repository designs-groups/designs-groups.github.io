Licence and conditions of use

The Designs and Groups: constructions and classifications database, including its data files, summaries, tables, and associated documentation, is made available under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International licence (CC BY 4.0).

The database may be copied, redistributed, adapted, transformed, and used in derivative works, including for commercial purposes, subject to the attribution requirements stated below.

Attribution and citation

The database is made available under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International licence (CC BY 4.0). Reuse must therefore provide appropriate attribution in accordance with the licence.

If you have used Designs and Groups in the preparation of a paper, preprint, thesis, presentation, software package, derived dataset, or other scholarly work, please cite the database as follows:

Seyed Hassan Alavi and Ashraf Daneshkhah, Designs and Groups: constructions and classifications (database), In preparation. https://designs-groups.github.io/

For scholarly use of particular designs, constructions, or classification results, please also cite the relevant original references where applicable.

Conditions for redistribution and adaptation

When material from the database is shared or adapted, users must retain appropriate credit, provide a reference to the CC BY 4.0 licence, and indicate whether changes were made. Attribution must not be presented in a way that suggests endorsement by the authors or by Bu-Ali Sina University.

Derived datasets or modified versions should clearly identify the source material and distinguish the authors' original data from subsequent modifications, corrections, or extensions.

Accuracy and scholarly use

The authors aim to maintain accurate mathematical and computational data, but the database is an evolving research resource. Users are responsible for independently checking results when they are used in proofs, classifications, software, or other work for which correctness is critical.

Suspected errors, inconsistencies, broken files, and missing scholarly references should be reported to alavi.s.hassan@gmail.com and alavi.s.hassan@basu.ac.ir.
